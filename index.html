Welcome to this experiment on word memorization using the Method of Loci (memory palace).

The Method of Loci is an ancient mnemonic strategy based on spatial memory. It involves associating abstract information (such as a list of words) with familiar physical locations (like the rooms of your house or steps of your daily commute). By anchoring new memories into these visual "slots," the brain can store and retrieve information more easily, and crucially, in the exact sequence.

To put it into practice, follow these steps:

Choose your palace: Mentally visualize a room you know by heart, such as your familly house, and establish a logical path step by step.

Place each word: As each word appears, mentally "deposit" it at the next location on your route. Try to create a vivid, unusual image.

Walk through for recall: When it's time to write the list, mentally retrace your steps — the word you left at each location will naturally come back to you.

You will see 20 words appear one by one. A distractor task will follow, after which you will recall the words.
